🌡️ Warm-Up
5 min
Arm circles + shoulder rolls
1 min
Forward and backward. Start small, get bigger. Prepares the rotator cuff for hanging and punching.
Hip hinge drill (bench)
1 min
Elbows on bench, wide stance, soft knees. Drive hips BACK until you feel the hamstring stretch, then thrust forward and squeeze glutes hard at the end. Slow here — you're waking up the posterior chain, not loading it yet.
Bodyweight squats
1 min
Slow and deep. 1 sec pause at the bottom. Getting blood into the quads and glutes before load.
Dead hang + shoulder shrugs
1 min
Hang relaxed 20 sec, then shrug shoulders toward ears and hold 2 sec. Activates the serratus and lats before loaded pulling.
Skip rope or high knees
1 min
Skip if you can, high knees if not — same stimulus. Just lifting HR to ~70% before the work starts. Don't worry about skipping technique yet.

⚡ Core & Posterior Chain
5 min
Hip hinge — loaded
3 × 15
Now with 10kg vest. Elbows on bench, wide stance, soft knees. Drive hips BACK — feel the hamstring load — then thrust forward and squeeze glutes hard. This is the same movement as a kettlebell swing or Romanian deadlift. It directly mimics the hip mechanics of moving uphill under load. 45 sec rest between sets.
Hanging knee raises
3 × 12
2kg ankle weights. Control both the raise and the lower. After the ladder your grip will be wrecked — that's the point. Core + grip endurance together.
Plank hold
2 × 45 sec
Vest on. Elbows or hands — elbows are harder. Squeeze everything: glutes, abs, quads. Breathe slowly. Anti-rotation core stability is critical for load-carrying on steep mountain terrain.
25:00
🏔️ Cardio Finisher
3 min
Skip rope or high knees
3 × 40 sec / 20 sec rest
Vest on. If skipping: aim for fast singles or double-unders if you can. If high knees: pump arms hard, drive knees to hip height. You should not be able to hold a conversation. This is the VO2max spike.
Weighted step-ups
40 sec fast pace
Chair, bench, or stairs. Vest on + 4kg ankles. Drive through the heel of the stepping leg. This directly simulates alpine approach terrain with a loaded pack.
28:00
❄️ Cool-Down
2 min
Dead hang
2 × 30 sec
Pure decompression. Let spine and shoulders fully relax. Breathe slowly through the nose. HR drops fast.
Hip hinge stretch (slow)
1 min
Same bench position but no load, slow pace. Drive hips back and hold 5 sec at the stretch. Hamstrings and glutes will be tight — this prevents next-day soreness.
Box breathing
5 rounds
Inhale 4 sec → hold 4 sec → exhale 4 sec → hold 4 sec. Activates the parasympathetic system. At altitude, when you're hypoxic and your body is panicking, this is what keeps you moving when others stop. Train it now so it's automatic.
MOUNTAINEERING NOTES
→Do this 2–3x per week, not daily. Alternate with strength days.
→The hip hinge is the most transferable exercise here — it's the exact mechanics of cramponing uphill under load.
→Skip rope even badly still builds foot coordination useful on technical terrain.
→Zone 2 cardio (long slow hikes with vest) builds the aerobic base this HIIT sits on top of.
→Box breathing practiced here becomes automatic at altitude when you're hypoxic.
